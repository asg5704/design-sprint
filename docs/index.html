Maryland Food Bank Design Sprint

Chalkboard Image

What is our Goal?

Answering Maryland Food Bank's critical problems trying to help the Food Bank generate more online donations through design, prototyping and validating feedback with it's users. Our team — Anna, Ashton, and myself — over the course of five days determined the best course of action was a design sprint!

Step One

Understand the Problem

The single most important thing to do: Understanding the problem. Step 1 of the Design Sprint, we hyper-focused on a very specific problem. Maryland Foodbank wanted a way to generate more online donations.

Our teams goals was to decid on ways to alleviate the stresses with online donations while maximizing a user-friendly UI.

Step Two

Diverge or Sketching

In Step 2 of the sprint, our team collaborated on different ideas known as 'Crazy 8s'. Our team began creating 8 different sketches to brainstorm. Eventually voting on the best features and ideas within our teams.

Each team member bringing their unique elements into the design sketching phase.

Step Three

Decide & Determine

Step 3 involves improving ideas within our team of 3. We refine our 'Crazy 8s' sketches down to the 'Design Trinity' fabricating and refining our new product within 3 sketches.

In the end, the team decided on an uncomplicated user interface (UI) for users to navigate the donations and volunteer pages for the Maryland Food Bank.

Step Four

Prototyping

The next step in the design sprint is the prototyping process. Demanding that the team develop a rough draft of the site or web app using an called Marvel. Which allows to create functionality by taking pictures of your mockups.

I contributed two design elements to our intuitive user-interface that was effortless to navigate and straight-forward in understanding for our users.

Step Five

Validate & Measure

Step 5 requires that the rough draft of our prototype is validated through user testing. Also included is measuring and refining the prototype, iterating until the prototype is a working product.

I contributed by creating a script for the interviewer to use as a basis for our validation process. Also serving as a note taker during the UX process.

During our user feedback we noticed core problems with our site. The users wanting more control with the mobility throughout the site coupled with the necessity of more information concerning the payment process.
