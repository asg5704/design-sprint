The Design Sprint

What is a design sprint?

The sprint is a five-day process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping and testing ideas with customers.

--Google Ventures

Step 1 - Understand the Problem

The single most important thing to do, understand the problem. Step 1 of the Design Sprint, we hyper-focused on a very specific problem. Maryland Foodbank wanted a way to generate more online donations.

The team decided on the ways to alleviate the stresses with online donations while maximizing a user-friendly UI.

Step 2 - Diverge or Sketching

In Step 2 of the sprint, our team collaborated on different ideas in term known as 'Crazy 8s'. Which is basically creating 8 different sketches for brainstorming. Eventually voting on the best features and ideas within our teams.

Each team member bringing their unique elements into the design sketching phase.

Step 3 - Decide and Determine

Step 3 involves improving ideas within our teams of 3-4 people. We refine our 'Crazy 8s' sketches down to the 'Design Trinity' and design a new feature in 3 sketches.

In the end our team decided on an easy to navigate page for the Maryland FoodBank donations page.

Step 4 - Prototyping

The next step in the design sprint is the prototyping process. This demands that the team develop a rough draft of the site or web app.

Designing an intuitive user-interface that was easy to navigate and straight-forward in understanding for our users.

Step 5 - Validate & Measure

Step 5 requires that the rough draft of our prototype is validated through user testing. Also included is measuring and refining the prototype, iterating until the prototype is a working product.

During our user feedback we noticed core problems in the users control and wanting to navigate backwards between pages.
